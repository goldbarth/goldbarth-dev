---
title: "What counts as an answer"
date: 2026-07-11
teaser: "An open question that has not found an experiment yet, and would not survive being forced into one."
---

Something keeps showing up across all of these and does not belong to any of
them: I have no working definition of when a system has answered a question.

Retrieval measures whether the right chunk came back. Grounding measures whether
the sentences trace to the chunks. Both can pass while the person who asked
walks away with nothing they can use, because they wanted to know whether to do
the thing, and the system told them what the document says about the thing.

The obvious move is to measure usefulness, and the obvious problem is that
usefulness is not a property of the answer. It is a property of the pair of the
answer and whoever asked, and the second half is not in the system.

I do not have a version of this that turns into a measurement yet, which is why
it is a note and not an experiment. Writing it down so it stops taking up space.
